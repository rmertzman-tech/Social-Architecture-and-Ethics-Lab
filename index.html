Welcome, System Architect

In this lab, you aren't just reading about ethics; you are engineering the conditions that make ethics possible.

How to use this app:

  1. Monitor the Lambda: This is your social "health bar." If it falls, your agents can no longer cooperate.
  2. Watch for Truncation: When agents are too stressed, they "truncate." They lose their unique personality and act on basic tribal instincts.
  3. Test the Sapolsky Limit: Slowly increase Entropy. Note the point where the bars suddenly shrink. This is the biological breaking point of morality.
  4. Deploy BioBonds: Use the green button to "cool" the environment and see if agents can regrow their depth.

Definitions

CIB (Coherent Information Bit)
The internal "blueprint" of a person's values. Deep CIBs are hard-won virtues[cite: 3, 498].
Entropy
Noise, chaos, and stress. In ethics, high entropy makes it physically harder to think clearly[cite: 1, 15, 412].
Sapolsky Limit
The point where biological stress (hormones) shuts down the "thinking" brain and forces us into "survival mode"[cite: 203, 528].
Truncation
When an agent "sheds" their complex identity and reverts to basic, often tribal, instincts to save energy[cite: 432, 550].
BioBond
An institutional "cooling system" (like a safety net) that protects agents from hitting their Sapolsky Limit[cite: 448, 625].
